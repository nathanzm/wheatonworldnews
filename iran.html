Iran Protects Secret Nuclear Program by Shifting Headquarters to a New Site

Updated October 8, 2014

Iran has shifted the location of its underground program to produce a nuclear warhead at a site opposite hospital in Tehran. The move has been completed since the imposition of sanctions on the body Organisation of Defensive Innovation and Research by the US Treasury in August (according to the Iranian opposition group National Council of Resistance of Iran). Its work was previously carried out at a military facility on the Parchin base in the Tehran suburbs. Tehran has repeatedly refused to open up to international inspectors in this said facility. This site was the location of a mysterious explosion last week that reportedly shook windows up to 16 miles away. This explosion revived security concerns in Iran so that foreign countries had revived a covert campaign to disrupt Iran’s underground nuclear work. Dangerously enough, five scientists were killed at the peak of a previous sabotage campaign between 2010 and 2012. The overhaul of operations at the key research body in charge of advanced technology necessary to manufacture a nuclear bomb has been orchestrated by Mohsen Fakhrizadeh, a powerful figure behind the scenes who is subject to US and UN sanctions. The opposition report yesterday said his organization’s main headquarters was now working from a building opposite the Chamran Hospital over a mile away from its previous location in Parchin. In an effort to provide additional protection to its leader, Fakhrizadeh was working in a separate office only over a block away. It was also said that “non-sensitive” sections of the group remain at the old site. Despite the impasse between the West and Iran over the secret weapons program, John Kerry, the US Secretary of State, said yesterday that he would meet his Iranian counterpart Mohammad Javad Zarif and Catherine Ashton, the EU representative, to secure an agreement in nuclear talks in Vienna for the next week. The negotiations have reached a key phase, as the deadline for the suspension of some sanctions on Iran expires in late November.
